# Hebrew Signs — Proverbs 31 Acrostic Test

**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Phase:** 4c (Third Acrostic Validation)
**Authors:** Daniel (framework originator), Hannah (analysis)

---

## The Test

Three alphabetic acrostics, three emotional registers:

| Text | Register | Question |
|------|----------|----------|
| Psalm 119 | Devotion | Do the dynamics map to meditation on Torah? |
| Lamentations 1 | Grief | Do the same dynamics map to grief with reversed polarity? |
| **Proverbs 31:10-31** | **Embodied Virtue** | **Do the dynamics map to a portrait of a woman in action?** |

If the letter dynamics are structural forces that shape expression regardless of content, all three should hold. Proverbs 31:10-31 is the most demanding test: it describes concrete physical action (weaving, buying fields, feeding households) rather than abstract devotion or communal grief.

## Results: Proverbs 31:10-31 Verse-by-Verse

### The 6Q Skeleton

| # | Letter | Dynamic | Proverbs 31 | Evidence |
|---|--------|---------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | א Aleph | **WHO** / Origination | v10: "**Who** can find a worthy woman? Her value is far above rubies." | The poem literally opens with WHO (מי). The originating question that establishes the subject. |
| 4 | ד Dalet | **WHERE** / Passage | v13: "She **seeks** wool and flax, and works eagerly with her hands." | דרשה (darsha) — the seeking that moves through thresholds. Spatial movement toward materials. |
| 19 | ק Qof | **WHEN** / Summons | v28: "Her children rise up and **call** (קרא) her blessed. Her husband also praises her." | The summons. The moment when recognition arrives — when praise comes forth. |
| 22 | ת Tav | **WHY** / Completion | v31: "**Give** (תנו) her of the fruit of her hands! Let her works praise her in the gates!" | WHY this woman matters. The final seal. Purpose fulfilled. Her works speak at the end. |

**Bet (WHAT):** v11: "The heart of her husband trusts **in** her" — trust contained within. What she is: the form that holds.
**Gimel (HOW):** v12: "She does him good... **all the days** of her life" — the sustained process. How she traverses time.

## Letter-Organ Alignments

Three verses contain the letter's physical organ in the Hebrew text:

| Verse | Letter | Organ | Hebrew |
|-------|--------|-------|--------|
| 19 | י Yud | hand | ידיה (yadeha) — "her hands" (doubled) |
| 20 | כ Kaf | palm | כפיה (kappeha) — "her palms" |
| 26 | פ Pe | mouth | פיה (piha) — "her mouth" |

These are not coincidences imposed by the reader. The acrostic constraint forces the verse to start with the letter; the poet then chose words that feature that letter's physical referent. The organ IS the dynamic made flesh.

---

## Verdict

The skeleton holds across a third register. Proverbs 31 is the most physically concrete of the three acrostics — weaving, buying fields, feeding children — and the letter dynamics map as cleanly as they did to abstract meditation (Psalm 119) or communal grief (Lamentations 1).

The letter-organ alignments (Yud-hand, Kaf-palm, Pe-mouth) are particularly compelling because they represent the poet's deliberate choice to feature the letter's physical referent within the acrostic constraint. The ancient poet knew what the letters meant.
